Meditation Beyond the Yoga Mat


What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

The person I chose to have a "mindful conversation" with was my sister. She loves talking and has naturally great EQ so seemed like the perfect "experimental subject". We started off with her talking and me listening. I found the 3 minutes of uninterrupted listening fairly hard purely because questions naturally come up and it's difficult not to ask them or form a discussion. She definitely enjoyed free reign of my attention and appreciated being listened to. When it was my turn to talk I couldn't think of much to say. I find too much attention off-putting and start to second guess the way I'm saying things. We both thought it was a useful exercise and would be a great way to resolve conflict but has it's awkward points if you're not totally comfortable with the environment or other person.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I really like the idea of mindful walking. Walking in general is relaxing and already a well known way to clear your head, so it makes sense that being mindful of each step would accentuate the whole process. I also love eating and often take food for granted (sorry food, I love and appreciate you really), so pretending each meal is an expensive, fancy, rare meal seems like it would fit well with me.
